5 steps of KonMari Method

As the end of the year is approaching, it is also a busy day for people to remove old clothes and replace many old things. The big cleaning can not only give the home a new look but also the refreshing space has a sense of tranquility and comfort. According to a Japanese organizing guru Marie Kondo, she emphasizes that "the ultimate goal of organizing is to learn to appreciate everything you have."

Kondo Marie shares that when cleaning or tidying up, "we should not focus on what to clear, but think about what to preserve and what should really be left. If people can appreciate what they have instead of always thinking about buying other items constantly, they will find that many things are actually unnecessary.‚Äù

She mentions that sometimes people often think, "why don't you put things when there is space?" In fact, this concept is wrong, because using the environment efficiently will make people feel more refreshed.

Below are the KonMari Method in 5 Steps:

Step 1: Think about your needs
Whenever helping people with housework, Kondo Marie must first ask the other person "what kind of life do you want?" Many people may be able to say it right away, but some people actually don't know what they want. However, this is exactly Marie Kondo wants people to think about what they really need, so that they can tell the difference between what they want and what they don't.

Step 2: Take out all similar items
The key to Marie's tidying magic is that tidying is not carried out on a room-by-room basis, but on a category-by-item basis, such as clothes, books, tableware, etc. For example, take out all the clothes and pile them up into a small mountain, and you will know how many clothes you have. "When organizing, we should organize according to the type of items, not the location." Kondo Marie said that there are five major categories of storage, namely "clothes", "books", "paper", "small things and sundries". " and "Emotional Items".

Step 3: Choose the object that is exciting
With the process of step 1, facing the mountain of items piled up in step 2, you can choose the items that make your heart beat.

Step 4: Thank and Discar
Keep what you love and be grateful for what you discard. Why give thanks for what is already about to be thrown away? Marie actually hopes that everyone will always take care of their own intention during the storage process. Desired items should be kept continuously, because it is necessary to create future life and life attitude; and the reason why people are reluctant to throw away items or feel tired of throwing things is actually because they do not realize that there must be a consciousness of "separating" from items, that is, the process of convincing yourself to "discard". Therefore, Marie Kondo will lead people to express gratitude for the items that will be discarded. She even knelt in the middle of the room and expressed her gratitude for everything and the house itself. People have to remind themselves of the relationship they have already formed with their surroundings, so that they can cut it off and move on when necessary.

Step 5: Give your favorite item a home
Since the items left behind make your heart move, it means that it has a certain status in your heart, so it should be properly stored and given a certain amount of respect. For example, it should not be randomly stacked and thrown away, or even put into a garbage bag. Treat the items that make your heart casually, and the next time you see it, you will lose the feeling of heart, and the life you want to create will not come.
